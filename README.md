The meditators have a high amount of broadband gamma and narrowband gamma when compared to controls. In this study we are investigating whether broadband gamma and slow gamma (narrowband) in fronto-temporal and occipital have any influence to establish a difference in neural activity of meditators and controls. To  analyse the influence, coherence is used to understand synchronization between distant electrodes. Using this the coherence sync between broadband gamma in fronto temporal and slow gamma in occipital can be analysed. As a result, integrated affective functions can be measured. Initially, in M2 protocol the global spatial coherence is under analysis to know the average possible connection and strength of phase relationship (PPC) which helps to measure through which average synchrony can be analysed.  Out of 8 stages of the task the M2 protocol has been chosen as the stimulus free gamma (broadband) through meditation and the stimulus induced gamma (slow gamma) through grating happen to occur together, and they can be analysed to find the which causes the difference in neural activity of meditators and controls.
